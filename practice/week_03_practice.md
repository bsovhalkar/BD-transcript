# Week 3

## 1. Short Notes
- Business: activity of developing, producing, selling goods/services for revenue and profit.
- Industry: group of firms making close substitutes (Porter's definition).
- Kotler's market-oriented view is strategically superior to product-oriented view.
- Three proofs for first success: **Proof of Concept, Proof of Product, Proof of Business**.
- BD pathway: Choose & prove product → sales cycle → revenue → reinvest → new markets/products.
- Three essentials: **Core Competence** (Tech+Product+Management) | **Niche** (Product+Market+Business) | **Strategic Skill** (Vision+Focus+Passion).
- Diversification: One Product-One Customer → Multiple Products-Multiple Customers.
- P/E ratios vary widely within the same industry (competitive strategy, not just sector membership, matters).
- **Keywords:** P/E ratio, core competence, value chain, acquisitions, diversification, proof of concept.
- **Associations:** Market-oriented definition → broader strategic choices → more BD opportunities.
