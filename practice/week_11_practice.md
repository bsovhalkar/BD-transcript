# Week 11

## 1. Short Notes
- Five generic industry environments: **Fragmented, New/Emerging, Transiting to Maturity, Declining, Global**.
- Three generic competitive strategies: **Cost Leadership, Differentiation, Focus/Niche**.
- **Ansoff Matrix**: Market Penetration (lowest risk) | Product Development | Market Development | Diversification (highest risk).
- PLC + Competitive Turbulence sub-phase; disruptive technologies restart the PLC.
- Innovation types: **Product, Process, Marketing Innovation** — all alter Five Forces.
- Industry evolution triggers: market-related, technology-related, strategy-related.
- **Label-driven growth**: own label/outlet → multi-channel → sub-contract → multi-branded.
- Nine strategic marketing dimensions (specialization, branding, push/pull, channel, quality, technology, price, service, finance).
- Growth entry conditions: positive NPV, competitive advantage, better than next best alternative.
- **Keywords:** Ansoff, PLC, generic strategies, industry evolution, fragmented industry, niche vs. mass, pioneer vs. follower.
- **Associations:** Ansoff risk increases as market and product unfamiliarity increases. PLC disruption = new market entry opportunity.
