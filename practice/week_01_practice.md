# Week 1

## 1. Short Notes
- **Business Development (BD)** = strategic activity that creates, nurtures, and expands businesses — broader than sales or marketing.
- Three core activities: **Sales** (transaction execution) | **Marketing** (market positioning, brand, communication) | **BD** (strategy, partnerships, new markets, long-term growth).
- BD operates at three levels: **Corporate** (portfolio), **Business Unit** (competitive strategy), **Functional** (marketing, ops, HR).
- Porter's Five Forces: Threat of New Entrants, Bargaining Power of Suppliers, Bargaining Power of Buyers, Threat of Substitutes, Industry Rivalry.
- Product Life Cycle (PLC): Introduction → Growth → Maturity → Decline.
- PESTEL: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal.
- Key BD metrics: Revenue, Market Share, Customer Lifetime Value, EBITDA.
- Ansoff Matrix: Market Penetration | Product Development | Market Development | Diversification.
- **Keywords:** Value chain, competitive advantage, STP (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning), core competence, SWOT.
- **Associations:** BD ≠ sales. BD integrates strategy + marketing + partnerships + innovation to create durable growth.
