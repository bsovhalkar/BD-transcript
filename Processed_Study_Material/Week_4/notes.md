## 🔍 Detailed Analysis

### 3.1 Markets and Industries — The Fundamental Distinction

**Market (Definition):**  
An aggregation of individuals or organisations who are *interested in and willing to buy* a product or service to obtain benefits that fulfil a particular want or need, **and who have the resources to buy**.

> *"There are three-four underlying concepts — interest, willingness, a purpose (benefits), and resources. All four must be present for a market to exist."* — Prof. Rao

**Industry (Definition):**  
A group of companies that conduct the same or similar businesses

### 3.2 Mobility Markets and Buyer Clusters

Markets fulfilling a single purpose are made up of **several buyer clusters with varied needs**. Within the macro "Mobility" market:
- **Personal Mobility** (2-wheelers, passenger cars)
- **Family Mobility** (3-wheelers, small buses)
- **Business Mobility** (staff transport vehicles)
- **Group Mobility** (large buses)
- **Off-Road Mobility** (4×4, construction vehicles)
- **Standby Mobility** (reserved fleet vehicles)

The Multi-Layered Market Framework A company's market is not a single entity but a series of concentric circles, each representing a different level of accessibility and engagement. Understanding these layers is crucial for developing a scalable market outreach strategy. Potential Market (The Universe): The total set of customers who show some level of interest in a market offer but may lack sufficient income or access to buy it. (19:49) Example: All financially savvy individuals for the stock market. Available Market (The Qualified): A subset of the potential market comprising customers who have a compelling interest and sufficient income and access to purchase the offer. (20:36) Target Market (The Pursuit): The specific part of the available market that a company decides to pursue based on its strategic goals, resources, and operational capabilities. (21:02) Case Study (Hospira): From an available market of 150+ countries, Hospira targeted a specific set (EU, Canada, etc.), deliberately excluding others like Russia, to focus its resources. (23:19) Secured Market (The Customer Base): The set of customers who are currently buying the company's product or service. This is the company's core and the starting point for expansion. (21:47) Case Study (Hospira): Hospira's secured market was the USA, where it had over 100 generic drug filings and a $3 billion turnover. ## ✅ Key Takeaways & Frequently Asked Points

*These concepts and facts appear most frequently in assignments and mock exams:*

1. Demand Cascade: funnel from Total Potential to **Actual Sales**
2. Disruption forecasting: **Expert opinions with simulations**
3. Analogy: for **new products with analogous comparables**
4. Test marketing: **Fully finished product**
5. Premium pricing: **Differentiated firms only**
6. Adoption: Innovators → Early Adopters → Early/Late Majority → Laggards
