### 1.4 Differences Between Sales, Marketing, and Business Development

This is one of the most critical distinctions in the course. The three functions are **not** synonymous — they differ in essence, orientation, scope, methodology, and metrics.


> **Key insight:** Sales is the narrowest in scope and most transactional. Marketing is broader and customer-centric. Business Development is the widest and most strategic, seeking to create lasting, mutually reinforcing relationships.

---

### 1.5 The Selling Process

The selling process is a fairly standardised, sequential process between a seller and a buyer in a marketplace:

```
Prospecting → Initial Contact → Qualifying Lead → Approaching Customer →
Product/Service Demonstration → Proposal and Clarification →
Sale Closing → Cash Collection → Repeat Contact
```

> **Judged by:** Its ability to generate sustainable revenues and profits from target groups.
