Detailed Analysis: From Local Production to Global Networks The modern value chain has evolved from a fully integrated, in-house model to a disaggregated, global network. Understanding these strategic options is crucial for business development. The Evolution of Manufacturing Strategy: Maximum In-house Manufacture: The traditional model where a company does everything itself. Captive Ancillary Manufacture: Outsourcing to dedicated, dependent suppliers. Make-or-Buy Decisions: A strategic choice to produce a component in-house or source it externally based on cost and quality. National Outsourcing: Sourcing from specialized domestic firms. Global Offshoring: The modern standard, involving sourcing or producing from anywhere in the world to maximize competitiveness. A Framework for Globalization Models: Companies combine production/sourcing strategies with their market focus to create distinct business models. For the Local Market: Traditional Domestic Model: On-shore production for the local market. (e.g., Early Indian industries). Local Make-or-Buy Model: On-shore outsourcing to enhance competitiveness in the local market. Manufacturing Competitiveness Model: Offshore production (at your own facility) to create cost-competitive products for your home market (e.g., An MNC's export-only unit in India). Strategic Sourcing Model: Offshore outsourcing to bring in cheaper components/products for the local market. For the Global Market: Traditional Globalization Model: On-shore production for export. ## ✅ Key Takeaways & Frequently Asked Points

*These concepts and facts appear most frequently in assignments and mock exams:*

1. Outsourcing = **third party** | Offshoring = **another country**
2. Basic Research: **pure knowledge, very long timeline**
3. Win-Win: **High concern for BOTH self and others**
4. Cultural Blinds: **mindsets blocking cross-cultural interaction**
5. Primary activities include: R&D, Manufacturing, Logistics, Sales, After-Sales
6. Apex Leadership: **Wisdom**
