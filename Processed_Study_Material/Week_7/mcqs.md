### Q11. A brand is most accurately defined as:

- **A)** A company's logo and color scheme
- **B)** The total experience, perception, and promise that a company represents in customers' minds
- **C)** The registered trademark of a product
- **D)** The product quality certification

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)** The total experience, perception, and promise that a company represents in customers' minds

**Explanation:** A brand is far more than a logo — it is the complete set of perceptions, emotions, and expectations held by customers.

**Difficulty:** Easy

</details>

---


### Q12. Brand equity is best described as:

- **A)** The book value of brand-related assets on the balance sheet
- **B)** The premium value a brand adds over and above a generic equivalent
- **C)** Total advertising spend on a brand
- **D)** The number of brand extensions launched

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)** The premium value a brand adds over and above a generic equivalent

**Explanation:** Brand equity is the incremental value a brand name adds to a product versus an unbranded equivalent.

**Difficulty:** Medium

</details>

### Q14. A brand extension strategy involves:

- **A)** Launching a completely new brand for a new product category
- **B)** Using an established brand name to enter a new product category
- **C)** Reducing the brand portfolio to focus on one core brand
- **D)** Rebranding after a company merger

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)** Using an established brand name to enter a new product category

**Explanation:** Brand extension leverages existing brand equity by applying the trusted name to a new product category.

**Difficulty:** Easy

</details>

### Q15. A house of brands strategy refers to:

- **A)** One master brand for all product lines
- **B)** Multiple independent brands each with distinct identities under one parent company
- **C)** Joint branding with competitor companies
- **D)** Licensing brand names to franchisees

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)** Multiple independent brands each with distinct identities under one parent company

**Explanation:** House of brands (e.g., P&G) maintains separate brand identities (Tide, Pampers, Gillette) under one parent.

### Q20. Brand awareness as the first step in brand equity means:

- **A)** Customers remember exact product specifications
- **B)** Customers can recognize or recall the brand when the category is mentioned
- **C)** Customers have purchased the brand at least once
- **D)** The brand has achieved >50% market share

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)** Customers can recognize or recall the brand when the category is mentioned

**Explanation:** Brand awareness = recognition or recall. It is the necessary foundation for all other brand equity components.

**Difficulty:** Easy

</details>

### Q21. Which branding challenge is MOST specific to the pharmaceutical industry?

- **A)** Need for consistent packaging design
- **B)** Regulatory constraints on promotional claims and mandatory generic substitution
- **C)** Managing retail shelf space
- **D)** Running television advertisements

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Explanation:** Pharma faces unique regulatory challenges — claims must be clinically proven and generic substitution may undermine brand loyalty.

### Q22. Which of the following is an example of a branded house architecture?
