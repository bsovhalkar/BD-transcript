Detailed Analysis: Segmentation & The Customer Journey 1. The Four Pillars of Consumer Market Segmentation Effective marketing requires slicing the market into meaningful groups. The lecture outlines four primary methods: Geographic: Dividing the market by physical location. Macro: National vs. International (e.g., a firm's home market vs. its export markets). Micro: Region, state, city, and the crucial urban vs. rural divide. In India, rural demand has shown incredible resilience and growth, sometimes outpacing urban markets, making it a key opportunity. Demographic: Segmenting based on quantifiable population statistics. Examples: Age, gender, income, education, life-cycle stage, culture, and generation (e.g., Millennials). The lecture highlights India's growing affluent middle class in its top 63 cities as a major engine of economic growth. Psychographic: Grouping customers based on psychological traits, lifestyle, and values. This answers why people buy. The VALS framework is a key tool, categorizing people into groups like "Thinkers," "Achievers," "Experiencers," and "Innovators" based on their motivations and resources. Behavioural: Segmenting based on a consumer's knowledge of, attitude towards, use of, or response to a product. Key factors include user reviews, herd behavior, disruptive trends (like COVID-19), and regulatory directions. This layer explains why a consumer's actual behavior might differ from their psychographic profile. 2.
