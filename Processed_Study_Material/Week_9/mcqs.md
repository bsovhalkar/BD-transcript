### Q11. A wholly-owned subsidiary is best defined as:

- **A)** A company where the parent holds exactly 50% of shares
- **B)** A company 100% owned by a parent company, with full strategic control
- **C)** A franchisee operating under a parent's brand
- **D)** A joint venture with equal partner ownership

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)** A company 100% owned by a parent company, with full strategic control

**Explanation:** A wholly-owned subsidiary is 100% owned by the parent, giving full strategic and operational control.

**Difficulty:** Easy

</details>

### Q17. A step-down subsidiary refers to:

- **A)** A subsidiary that is being wound down
- **B)** A subsidiary of a subsidiary — a second-level indirect holding
- **C)** A joint venture at the subsidiary level
- **D)** A subsidiary with reduced equity interest

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)** A subsidiary of a subsidiary — a second-level indirect holding

**Explanation:** Step-down subsidiaries are indirect holdings: Parent owns Sub A, which owns Sub B (step-down subsidiary of Parent).

### Q19. Which is a key reason why global companies use subsidiaries rather than direct branches for new markets?

- **A)** Subsidiaries require less capital investment

- **C)** Subsidiaries don't require local regulatory approvals
- **D)** Subsidiaries automatically receive tax exemptions
