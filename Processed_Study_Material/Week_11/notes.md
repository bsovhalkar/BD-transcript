### A. Cost Leadership

- Achieve the **lowest cost position** relative to all competitors.
- Grants either **pricing power** (undercut rivals) or **margin power** (higher profits at the same price).

### B. Differentiation

- Provide **superior features or value** relative to competitors.
- Grants the firm the ability to **charge a premium**.

### C. Focus / Niche

- Concentrate on a **specific customer segment** relative to the broader market.
- Yields a long-term sustainable advantage through deep specialisation.

> These are high-level prescriptions that must underpin specific, varied tactical actions in the real world.

Detailed Analysis Navigating the Competitive Landscape A firm's position in an industry is not random; it's a result of strategic choices about its core capabilities. We can map these choices on a grid of  Design Capability (Pioneering vs. Follower) and Production Capability (Wide vs. Narrow). Commoditized Companies: (Follower Design, Narrow Production) Profile: Low-margin, high-volume, focused on a limited customer base. Challenge: Intense price competition and low differentiation. Emerging Companies: (Pioneering Design, Narrow Production) Profile: High-margin, low-volume, driven by innovative technology. Challenge: Scaling production and market reach before competitors catch up. Diversified Mass-Market Companies: (Follower Design, Wide Production) Profile: Low-margin, high-volume, serving diverse markets by leveraging production scale. Common in emerging economies. Challenge: Maintaining efficiency and profitability against focused competitors. Industry "Gems": (Pioneering Design, Wide Production) Profile: The rare ideal—high-margin, high-volume companies with pioneering technology and diversified businesses. Challenge: Sustaining innovation to maintain high technological and mobility barriers. The critical strategic questions for any firm are: Where to move on this grid, how to move (e.g., through R&D investment, acquisition), and when to move. ## ✅ Key Takeaways & Frequently Asked Points

*These concepts and facts appear most frequently in assignments and mock exams:*

1. Competitive Turbulence: **Maturity** phase
2. Follower strategy: **market fragmentation, cost leadership, low returns**
3. Stuck: **fragmented + fails to evolve**
4. Endgame: Leadership, Niche, Harvesting, Divestment (**NOT Innovation**)
5. First-mover: **Introduction** phase
