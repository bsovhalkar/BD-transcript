### Q11. The primary distinction between a Customer and a Consumer is:

- **A)** Customers pay more than consumers
- **B)** A customer buys the product; a consumer uses the product
- **C)** Customers are always businesses; consumers are individuals
- **D)** Consumers generate revenue; customers do not

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)** A customer buys the product; a consumer uses the product

**Explanation:** Customer = buyer (purchase decision), Consumer = user (product experience).

**Difficulty:** Easy

</details>

---


### Q12. Which of the following best characterizes a B2B purchase?

- **A)** Impulse-driven and emotionally influenced
- **B)** Price-elastic with high volume of buyers
- **C)** Procedure-bound, multi-tiered, and based on objective parameters
- **D)** Heavily influenced by branding and packaging

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: C)** Procedure-bound, multi-tiered, and based on objective parameters

**Explanation:** B2B purchases involve formal procedures, multi-stakeholder decisions, and objective evaluation criteria.

**Difficulty:** Medium

</details>

### Q13. In a Business Market, demand for industrial products is typically:

- **A)** Highly price-elastic
- **B)** Derived from the demand for finished consumer goods
- **C)** Independent of consumer market trends
- **D)** Purely seasonal and cyclical

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B)** Derived from the demand for finished consumer goods

**Explanation:** Industrial demand is derived — demand for steel rises when demand for automobiles rises.

**Difficulty:** Medium

</details>

### Q22. B2B demand is typically described as inelastic because:
