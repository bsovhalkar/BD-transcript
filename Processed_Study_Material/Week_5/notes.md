## 🔍 Detailed Analysis

### 2.1 Customers vs. Consumers

- A **dealer** is a customer for an automobile firm; the dealer's end-buyers are the consumers.
- The company must understand both — the dealer (customer) and the end-user (consumer) — because **demand at the consumer level drives the entire marketing value chain**.
- In practice, the two terms are used interchangeably in strategic and business development courses.

### 2.3 Customer-Oriented Organisation

**Contemporary Concept:** A circular organisation where **every function** (R&D, Production, Finance, Marketing, IT, Others) is encircled by and directly connects with customers.

Market Structure & Access Consumer: Millions of buyers, easily accessible through retail, e- commerce, and direct-to-consumer (D2C) channels. Business: Fewer, more concentrated buyers. Access is difficult and requires formal processes like appointments, negotiations, and winning orders. Decision-Making & Influence Consumer: Decisions are often individual, impulsive, and heavily influenced by branding, packaging, promotion, and social circles. Business: Decisions are multi-tiered, procedure-bound, and based on objective parameters, testing, and long-term value. Explicit codes of conduct are common. Product & Demand Consumer: Products are standardized (e.g., toothpaste). Demand is highly elastic—lower prices can significantly increase volume. ## ✅ Key Takeaways & Frequently Asked Points

*These concepts and facts appear most frequently in assignments and mock exams:*

1. Customer = **buys** | Consumer = **uses**
2. B2B: **multi-tiered, procedure-bound, rational**
3. B2B demand is **derived** from consumer product demand
4. Inverted pyramid: **Customers at top**
5. Homepreneur = **home-based entrepreneur**
6. B2B demand: **inelastic** once product proven
