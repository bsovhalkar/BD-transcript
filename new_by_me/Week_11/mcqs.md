## 📝 Part A: Weekly Assignment Questions

> *Official assignment questions for this week. These directly test core concepts.*

### Q1. The skill and control over the design and manufacturing capabilities determine how  companies can achieve strategic fit with their:

- **A)** ​ Analysts
- **B)** ​ Shareholders
- **C)** ​ Strategic customers
- **D)** ​ Internal departments
> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) ​ Strategic customers**


### Q2. A company will be less vulnerable to new entrants and new technology-driven competition  if the company:

- **A)** ​ Has only financial strength
- **B)** ​ Is large, with a high market share
- **C)** ​ Is small, with a low market share
- **D)** ​ Continues on the path of continuous product and market improvement
> ✅ **Correct Answer: D) ​ Continues on the path of continuous product and market improvement**


### Q3. Which of the following is true?

- **A)** ​ Innovation can occur only in respect of product
- **B)** ​ Innovation can occur in any and all parts of a company’s value chain
- **C)** ​ Innovation can occur only in start-ups
- **D)** ​ Innovation cannot occur in marketing
> ✅ **Correct Answer: B) ​ Innovation can occur in any and all parts of a company’s value chain**


### Q4. One of the more recent improvements in lighting, which is also backed by Nobel  Prize-winning science and technology, has been:

- **A)** ​ LED lighting
- **B)** ​ LCD lighting
- **C)** ​ Fluorescent lighting
- **D)** ​ Incandescent lighting
> ✅ **Correct Answer: A) ​ LED lighting**


### Q5. Corning is a great example of global business growth based largely on one product category.  Corning’s core competence is in:

- **A)** ​ Processing of cobalt
- **B)** ​ Processing of glass
- **C)** ​ Processing of Lithium
- **D)** ​ Processing of Ion
> ✅ **Correct Answer: B) ​ Processing of glass**


### Q6. Which of the following integrates hardware and software of a device?

- **A)** ​ Timeliness objective
- **B)** ​ Market objective
- **C)** ​ Operating system
- **D)** ​ R&D objective
> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) ​ Operating system**


### Q7. 3D printing is also called:

- **A)** ​ Rapid printing
- **B)** ​ Deductive manufacturing
- **C)** ​ 360-degree printing
- **D)** ​ Additive manufacturing
> ✅ **Correct Answer: D) ​ Additive manufacturing**


### Q8. Roku Roku Sangyo of Japan deploys the following in its machine tools. That feature  provides competitive advantage for business development for the company:

- **A)** ​ Sensors and IoT
- **B)** ​ Tools
- **C)** ​ Machines
- **D)** ​ Machines with tools
> ✅ **Correct Answer: A) ​ Sensors and IoT**


### Q9. Firms in the fragmented industry must understand the following and plan their strategies  accordingly:

- **A)** ​ Market
- **B)** ​ Firm
- **C)** ​ Finance
- **D)** ​ Structural limitations which include the above and more
> ✅ **Correct Answer: D) ​ Structural limitations which include the above and more**


### Q10. New technologies, new business models, and hence new industries emerge all the time. An  example of this is:

- **A)** ​ Green hydrogen
- **B)** ​ mRNA vaccine technology
- **C)** ​ Chemical-free consumer products
- **D)** ​ All the above
> ✅ **Correct Answer: D) ​ All the above**

### Q1. Pfizer's rapid development of the COVID-19 vaccine was attributed to five key leadership qualities. Which of the following was NOT among them?
*🔴 Hard | Source: Lecture_W11L114_Growth_Examples*

- **A)** Foresight
- **B)** Persistence
- **C)** Risk aversion
- **D)** Courage

> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) Risk aversion**

### Q2. Which statement accurately compares 'Internal Development' and 'Acquisition' for entering a new business?
*🔴 Hard | Source: Lecture_W11L121_Global_Industries_and_New_Businesses*

- **A)** Internal development is faster and less costly than acquisition.
- **B)** Acquisition typically invites more immediate retaliation from incumbents than internal development.
- **C)** Internal development builds proprietary control but is slow and resource-intensive, while acquisition offers speed but integration risks.
- **D)** Acquisition avoids integration challenges and hidden liabilities, making it a lower-risk option.

> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) Internal development builds proprietary control but is slow and resource-intensive, while acquisition offers speed but integration risks.**

### Q3. Which of the following is NOT one of the four strategic alternatives for managing the 'endgame' in declining industries?
*🔴 Hard | Source: Lecture_W11L116_Mature_Industries_and_Declining_Industries*

- **A)** Leadership
- **B)** Niche
- **C)** Innovation
- **D)** Harvesting

> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) Innovation**

### Q4. According to the Product Life Cycle (PLC) model, when does profitability typically begin to decline, even while sales are still growing?
*🔴 Hard | Source: Lecture_W11L113_Growth_Strategies*

- **A)** During the Introduction Phase.
- **B)** At the onset of "Competitive Turbulence" during the Growth Phase.
- **C)** Throughout the Maturity Phase.
- **D)** During the Decline Phase.

> ✅ **Correct Answer: B) At the onset of "Competitive Turbulence" during the Growth Phase.**

### Q5. Which of the following is NOT typically a cause of fragmentation in an industry?
*🔴 Hard | Source: Lecture_W11L115_Fragmented_Industries_and_Emerging_Industries*

- **A)** Absence of economies of scale.
- **B)** High inventory costs favoring small-batch production.
- **C)** High entry barriers.
- **D)** Need for localized design or service.

> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) High entry barriers.**

### Q6. In managing the endgame of declining industries, which strategic alternative aims to maximize cash flow by systematically minimizing new investments while maintaining sales for as long as possible?
*🔴 Hard | Source: Lecture_W11L116_Mature_Industries_and_Declining_Industries*

- **A)** Leadership
- **B)** Niche
- **C)** Harvesting
- **D)** Quick Divestment

> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) Harvesting**

### Q7. In the Product Life Cycle (PLC), when does profitability typically begin to decline, even while sales are still growing?
*🟡 Medium | Source: Lecture_W11L113_Growth_Strategies*

- **A)** Introduction Phase
- **B)** Growth Phase (during competitive turbulence)
- **C)** Maturity Phase
- **D)** Decline Phase

> ✅ **Correct Answer: B) Growth Phase (during competitive turbulence)**

### Q8. Which of the following is a primary cause of fragmentation in an industry?
*🟡 Medium | Source: Lecture_W11L115_Fragmented_Industries_and_Emerging_Industries*

- **A)** High entry barriers.
- **B)** Absence of economies of scale.
- **C)** High inventory costs that favor large-batch production.
- **D)** Predominance of cost leadership strategies.

> ✅ **Correct Answer: B) Absence of economies of scale.**

### Q9. In the Product Life Cycle, when does 'Competitive Turbulence' typically occur?
*🟡 Medium | Source: Lecture_W11L113_Growth_Strategies*

- **A)** Introduction Phase
- **B)** Growth Phase
- **C)** Maturity Phase
- **D)** Decline Phase

> ✅ **Correct Answer: B) Growth Phase**

### Q10. What is a 'Stuck' Industry?
*🟡 Medium | Source: Lecture_W11L115_Fragmented_Industries_and_Emerging_Industries*

- **A)** An industry with high entry barriers and economies of scale.
- **B)** A fragmented industry where firms fail to evolve due to lack of resources or myopic outlook.
- **C)** A newly formed industry created by technological innovation.
- **D)** An industry where a small number of firms hold the majority of market share.

> ✅ **Correct Answer: B) A fragmented industry where firms fail to evolve due to lack of resources or myopic outlook.**

### Q11. What cultural approach is described as causing industries to wither and collapse due to rigid, introverted leadership and a focus on only what customers are used to?
*🟡 Medium | Source: Lecture_W11L114_Growth_Examples*

- **A)** Rationality of Innovation
- **B)** Design Thinking
- **C)** Technological Obduracy
- **D)** Market Segmentation

> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) Technological Obduracy**

### Q12. What is "strategic inertia" in mature industries?
*🟡 Medium | Source: Lecture_W11L116_Mature_Industries_and_Declining_Industries*

- **A)** A strategy to rapidly acquire competitors.
- **B)** A complacent belief that a stable position will last forever, leading to a failure to recognize threats.
- **C)** The process of systematically minimizing new investments to maximize cash flow.
- **D)** A strategy to enter new emerging industries.

> ✅ **Correct Answer: B) A complacent belief that a stable position will last forever, leading to a failure to recognize threats.**

### Q13. Which of the following is a disadvantage of entering a new business via acquisition?
*🟡 Medium | Source: Lecture_W11L121_Global_Industries_and_New_Businesses*

- **A)** Slow and capital-intensive process.
- **B)** Directly adds new capacity to the market, provoking retaliation.
- **C)** Significant integration challenges and potential for hidden liabilities.
- **D)** Avoids paying a premium for an existing business.

> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) Significant integration challenges and potential for hidden liabilities.**

### Q14. What is 'Harvesting' as a strategy for declining industries?
*🟢 Easy | Source: Lecture_W11L116_Mature_Industries_and_Declining_Industries*

- **A)** Investing heavily to become the dominant player in the shrinking market.
- **B)** Systematically minimizing new investments while maintaining sales for as long as possible to maximize cash flow.
- **C)** Identifying and defending a durable, profitable niche segment.
- **D)** Selling the business early in the decline phase to maximize recovery value.

> ✅ **Correct Answer: B) Systematically minimizing new investments while maintaining sales for as long as possible to maximize cash flow.**

### Q15. What is the primary goal of a 'Followership' strategy?
*🟢 Easy | Source: Lecture_W11L114_Growth_Examples*

- **A)** Market monopoly with high returns
- **B)** Market segmentation with healthy returns
- **C)** Market fragmentation with low returns, focused on cost leadership
- **D)** Being first to design and first to market

> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) Market fragmentation with low returns, focused on cost leadership**
