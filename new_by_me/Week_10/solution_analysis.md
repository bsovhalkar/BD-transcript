### Q1. Apple component supplier and assembly system is highly and predominantly focused on:
**Options:** a) European countries | b) Asian countries | c) USA | d) Japan

> ✅ **Correct Answer: b) Asian countries**

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
Apple's **global supply chain** is heavily concentrated in **Asian countries** — with Taiwan (TSMC for chips, Foxconn for assembly), China (Foxconn, Pegatron assembly), South Korea (Samsung/LG displays, memory), Japan (Sony camera sensors, Murata components), and increasingly India (Tata Electronics, Foxconn India) forming the backbone. While Apple designs in the US, over 90% of its supply chain and assembly is Asian-centric.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- **a) European countries** — Europe has minimal role in Apple's component supply chain.
- **c) USA** — Apple designs in the US but manufactures almost nothing there.
- **d) Japan** — Japan is part of the Asian supply chain but does not describe the entire predominantly Asian focus.

**Concepts Involved:**
- Global supply chain geography.
- BD in supply chain: partner selection and geographic concentration.
- China Plus One risk: over-reliance on a single country/region.

**Common Mistakes:**
- Choosing "Japan" because Japan has Apple suppliers — Japan is ONE part of the Asian supply chain, not the whole.

**Memory Aid:** *Apple = Designed in California, Made in Asia. Asian supply chain dominates the iPhone.*

### Q2. Adalimumab (Humira) medicine is:
**Options:** a) A synthetic chemical product | b) A biological product | c) A biosimilar product | d) A genetic product

> ✅ **Correct Answer: b) A biological product**

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
**Adalimumab (Humira)** is a **monoclonal antibody** — a large, complex biological molecule derived from living cells (CHO cells). It is a **biological product** (biologic), not a small molecule synthetic chemical. Biologics are produced through biotechnology (cell culture, fermentation) rather than chemical synthesis. Humira (by AbbVie) was the world's best-selling drug for many years, used for rheumatoid arthritis, Crohn's disease, and psoriasis.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- **a) Synthetic chemical** — Small molecule drugs (aspirin, paracetamol) are synthetic chemicals; Humira is a biologic.
- **c) Biosimilar** — Biosimilars are generic versions of biologics approved after patent expiry; Humira itself is the originator biologic.
- **d) Genetic product** — Not standard pharmaceutical terminology; monoclonal antibodies are biologics.

**Concepts Involved:**
- Biologics vs. small molecules: fundamental distinction in pharma.
- Monoclonal antibodies: targeted biological therapies.
- BD in pharma: biologic patent cliffs and biosimilar opportunity.

**Common Mistakes:**
- Confusing "biosimilar" with "biological" — Humira IS the original biologic; biosimilars are copies (e.g., Amjevita, Hyrimoz).

**Memory Aid:** *Humira = Huge molecule from living cells = Biologic. Not synthesised in a flask — grown in a bioreactor.*

### Q3. Operations strategy of a firm determines:
**Options:** a) Only capacity of supply | b) Only quality of supply | c) Only cost of supply | d) Capacity, quality and cost of supply

> ✅ **Correct Answer: d) Capacity, quality and cost of supply**

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
A firm's **operations strategy** is the comprehensive plan for how the company will configure its production, supply chain, and service delivery systems. It determines the **triple output** of operations:
1. **Capacity** — how much can be produced/supplied.
2. **Quality** — to what standards will output conform.
3. **Cost** — at what per-unit cost can the firm deliver.
All three are interrelated and jointly determined by operations strategy decisions (plant location, technology, workforce, processes).

**Why other options are wrong:**
- **a), b), c)** — Each represents only one dimension; operations strategy determines all three simultaneously.

**Concepts Involved:**
- Operations strategy: configuration of capacity, quality, cost.
- Trade-off triangle: Quality vs. Cost vs. Speed/Capacity.
- BD and operations: operational excellence as competitive advantage.

**Common Mistakes:**
- Thinking operations strategy only determines cost — in reality, it determines the full capability triangle of capacity, quality, AND cost.

**Memory Aid:** *Operations strategy = CQC: Capacity + Quality + Cost. The three pillars of operational competence.*

### Q4. In an organisation, passion and energy are best described as:
**Options:** a) Independent attributes with no influence on each other | b) Not connected with each other | c) Interrelated with each other | d) Mutually exclusive

> ✅ **Correct Answer: c) Interrelated with each other**

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
In organisational and leadership contexts, **passion** (deep commitment and love for one's work) and **energy** (physical and mental vitality to pursue goals) are deeply **interrelated** — passion fuels energy, and sustained energy reinforces passion. Together they create the self-perpetuating drive that characterises high-performing BD professionals. They are complementary forces, not independent or mutually exclusive variables.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- **a) Independent** — They actively influence each other; passion increases energy and vice versa.
- **b) Not connected** — Clearly incorrect; they are linked psychological constructs.
- **d) Mutually exclusive** — They coexist and reinforce each other; they are not alternatives.

**Concepts Involved:**
- BD competencies: personal attributes of BD professionals.
- Leadership energy and passion: drivers of BD performance.
- Human capital in BD: beyond skills to motivational attributes.

**Common Mistakes:**
- None expected; this is logically consistent — passion and energy are clearly interrelated in organisational behaviour.

**Memory Aid:** *Passion + Energy = self-reinforcing BD fuel. Passionate people have energy; energetic people stay passionate.*

### Q5. Negotiation between two partners seeks to develop an agreement in which:
**Options:** a) The dominant partner wins over the other | b) The weaker partner stalls till it gets its due | c) Both have a shared win-win agenda | d) Neither partner is a winner

> ✅ **Correct Answer: c) Both have a shared win-win agenda**

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
Effective **negotiation in BD** is not a zero-sum game where one party wins at the other's expense. A sustainable BD partnership requires a **win-win agreement** — where both parties perceive the outcome as fair, beneficial, and aligned with their goals. Win-lose negotiations produce resentment and unstable partnerships. The win-win principle is the foundation of long-term BD relationships.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- **a) Dominant partner wins** — This creates an unsatisfied, resentful partner who may exit or underperform.
- **b) Weaker partner stalls** — Stalling tactics create mistrust and damage the relationship.
- **d) Neither is a winner** — A no-win outcome defeats the purpose of negotiation.

**Concepts Involved:**
- Negotiation in BD: integrative vs. distributive bargaining.
- Win-win negotiation: BATNA, ZOPA, mutual gains.
- Partnership sustainability: both parties must perceive value.

**Common Mistakes:**
- Thinking negotiation = one side "wins" — in BD, the goal is mutual gain, not victory over the partner.

**Memory Aid:** *BD negotiation = Win-Win or No Deal. A deal where one side loses = a deal that doesn't last.*

### Q6. Every negotiation must have a pricing outcome. The zone of possible price negotiation:
**Options:** a) Can never be determined | b) Is never transparent | c) Lies between the Buyer's minimum price intention and the Seller's maximum price intention | d) None of the above

> ✅ **Correct Answer: c) Lies between the Buyer's minimum price intention and the Seller's maximum price intention**

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
In negotiation theory, the **Zone of Possible Agreement (ZOPA)** for price lies between:
- The **Buyer's maximum** willingness to pay (above which the buyer walks away).
- The **Seller's minimum** acceptable price (below which the seller walks away).
The final agreed price within this zone depends on the bargaining power, information, and negotiation skill of each party. This zone can be estimated through market intelligence and BATNA analysis.

**Note:** The option says "Buyer's minimum price intention and Seller's maximum price intention" which represents the boundaries from each party's perspective — the overlap of their acceptable ranges.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- **a) Can never be determined** — ZOPA can be estimated through market research and BATNA analysis.
- **b) Never transparent** — While private, ZOPA can be approximated by both parties.

**Concepts Involved:**
- ZOPA: Zone of Possible Agreement.
- BATNA: Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement.
- Price negotiation in BD partnerships.

**Common Mistakes:**
- Thinking ZOPA cannot be determined — experienced BD negotiators estimate it through research and intelligence gathering.

**Memory Aid:** *ZOPA = the sweet spot between what buyer will pay maximum and what seller will accept minimum.*

### Q7. The value chain of a company describes the functions and domains which can be used by a business development leader to:
**Options:** a) Shuffle them as an executive responsibility | b) Exit from them as a financial initiative | c) Understand and project the company's competencies | d) Reconstruct them as a CEO

> ✅ **Correct Answer: c) Understand and project the company's competencies**

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
**Porter's Value Chain** (Primary activities: Inbound Logistics, Operations, Outbound Logistics, Marketing & Sales, Service; Support activities: Firm Infrastructure, HR, Technology, Procurement) is a framework that BD leaders use to **understand the company's strengths (value points) and weaknesses (value gaps)** and to **project** these competencies to potential partners. It is the analytical foundation for identifying what the company brings to a BD partnership.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- **a) Shuffle responsibilities** — Value chain analysis is strategic, not an HR restructuring tool.
- **b) Exit activities** — Divestiture is a separate strategic decision; value chain analysis identifies competencies, not exit candidates per se.
- **d) Reconstruct as CEO** — Value chain analysis is for understanding and projecting competencies, not CEO-level structural reconstruction.

**Concepts Involved:**
- Porter's Value Chain: primary and support activities.
- BD application of value chain: competency identification and projection.
- Competitive advantage: value chain as the source of competitive differentiation.

**Common Mistakes:**
- Thinking value chain analysis is only for internal restructuring — its primary BD application is identifying competencies to build partnerships around.

**Memory Aid:** *Value Chain = Mirror showing your strengths. BD leader holds the mirror up to say "Here's what we bring to the table."*

### Q8. The following is an important aspect of culture and negotiation:
**Options:** a) Agreement form | b) Time sense | c) Objectivity | d) All the above

> ✅ **Correct Answer: d) All the above**

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
Cross-cultural negotiation is influenced by all three:
- **Agreement form**: Different cultures view agreements differently — some prefer detailed legal contracts (Western), others rely on relationship-based verbal commitments (some Asian cultures).
- **Time sense**: Some cultures are monochronic (strict schedules, punctuality = respect) while others are polychronic (flexible time, relationships over schedules).
- **Objectivity**: Some cultures focus on data and logic (low-context); others rely more on relationships and context (high-context) in decision-making.
All three are critical cultural dimensions affecting negotiation outcomes.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- **a), b), c)** — Each is a valid cultural aspect, but all three together are comprehensive.

**Concepts Involved:**
- Cross-cultural negotiation.
- Hofstede's cultural dimensions.
- High-context vs. low-context cultures.
- Time orientation in cultures.

**Common Mistakes:**
- None expected; "all the above" is clearly correct when all listed options are valid.

**Memory Aid:** *Cultural negotiation = ATOs: Agreement form + Time sense + Objectivity = three cultural blindspots to watch.*

### Q9. Japanese business etiquette requires that persons be addressed respectfully with suffix of:
**Options:** a) Esquire | b) Sayonara | c) Arigato | d) San

> ✅ **Correct Answer: d) San**

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
In Japanese business culture, the honorific suffix **"San"** (さん) is the standard respectful form of address — equivalent to Mr./Ms./Mrs. in English. Using it correctly demonstrates cultural respect and awareness, which is critical in Japanese BD relationships where respect and formality are highly valued. Addressing someone without "San" or using first names prematurely is considered disrespectful.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- **a) Esquire** — English/legal honorific, not Japanese.
- **b) Sayonara** — Japanese for "goodbye," not an honorific suffix.
- **c) Arigato** — Japanese for "thank you," not an honorific suffix.

**Concepts Involved:**
- Cross-cultural business etiquette: Japan.
- Japanese honorifics: San, Kun, Chan, Sama (in order of formality).
- Cultural competency in global BD.

**Common Mistakes:**
- Confusing Japanese words — "Sayonara" (goodbye) and "Arigato" (thank you) are common Japanese words that students may confuse with honorifics.

**Memory Aid:** *San = Japanese "Sir/Ma'am." Tanaka San = Mr./Ms. Tanaka. Arigato = Thank you. Sayonara = Goodbye.*

### Q10. In organisational and leadership contexts, a firm belief in the reliability, integrity, or ability of an individual or institution is referred to as:
**Options:** a) Stand | b) View | c) Opinion | d) Trust

> ✅ **Correct Answer: d) Trust**

**Step-by-step Explanation:**
**Trust** is defined as a firm belief in the reliability, integrity, or ability of someone or something. In BD contexts, trust is the foundation of all partnerships, alliances, and long-term customer relationships. Without trust, no BD structure can sustain itself — trust reduces transaction costs, enables open communication, and creates the conditions for mutual value creation.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- **a) Stand** — Refers to a position or stance on an issue.
- **b) View** — A perspective or opinion, not a commitment belief.
- **c) Opinion** — A belief or judgement, but less strong and committed than trust.

**Concepts Involved:**
- Trust in BD: the foundation of all long-term partnerships.
- Organisational trust: interpersonal and institutional.
- BD competency: trust-building as a leadership skill.

**Common Mistakes:**
- None; trust is clearly the most fitting term in BD/leadership contexts.

**Memory Aid:** *Trust = The invisible glue of BD. Without trust, no partnership holds. T.R.U.S.T. = The Real Underpinning of Sustainable Teamwork.*

## ✍️ Additional Practice Questions – Week 10

**PQ1.** Which of the following is a "Cultural Blind" that blocks effective cross-cultural interaction?
- A) Cultural curiosity
- B) Stereotyping
- C) Active listening
- D) Adapting communication style
> **Answer: B** | Stereotyping = assuming all people from a culture behave identically = cultural blind spot.

**PQ2.** What is BATNA in negotiation?
- A) The highest price a buyer is willing to pay
- B) The Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement — the option if negotiation fails
- C) The minimum acceptable quality standard
- D) A cultural negotiation style from South Asia
> **Answer: B** | BATNA = fallback option. Knowing your BATNA strengthens your negotiating position.

**PQ3.** The primary difference between a biologic and a small molecule drug is:
- A) Biologics are cheaper to manufacture
- B) Biologics are large, complex molecules derived from living cells; small molecules are chemically synthesised
- C) Small molecules require cell culture manufacturing
- D) Biologics are always administered orally
> **Answer: B** | Biologic = living cell-derived, large complex molecule. Small molecule = chemically synthesised.

**PQ4.** In which type of research is the objective pure knowledge acquisition with no immediate commercial application?
- A) Applied Research
- B) Developmental Research
- C) Basic Research
- D) Market Research
> **Answer: C** | Basic Research = pure knowledge, long timeline, no immediate commercial goal. (Appeared 2× in mock exams.)

**PQ5.** A BD professional's value chain analysis should primarily focus on:
- A) Identifying which departments to close down
- B) Understanding the company's strengths and projecting them to potential partners
- C) Calculating the financial value of each department
- D) Reorganising the company's hierarchy
> **Answer: B** | Value chain for BD = identify competencies (value points) and gaps → project to partners → form complementary alliances.
