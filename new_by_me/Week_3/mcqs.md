### Q1. What is the 'Entry Deterrent Price' in Porter's Five Forces Theory?
*🔴 Hard | Source: Lecture_W3L30_Porter_s_Five_Forces_Theory*

- **A)** The lowest price an incumbent can charge without incurring losses.
- **B)** The maximum price a new entrant can charge for a superior product.
- **C)** A strategic price point set by an incumbent firm to discourage new competitors from entering the market.
- **D)** The price at which a substitute product becomes more attractive than the original.

> ✅ **Correct Answer: C) A strategic price point set by an incumbent firm to discourage new competitors from entering the market.**

### Q2. According to the lecture, what is the 'Virtuous Cycle of Growth' for a business?
*🟡 Medium | Source: Lecture_W3L29_Industry_and_Business*

- **A)** Acquire competitors, increase market share, raise prices, reinvest.
- **B)** Prove the core, generate a cash pool, expand and diversify, iterate.
- **C)** Develop new products, launch globally, achieve economies of scale, become a conglomerate.
- **D)** Focus on R&D, patent innovations, license technology, collect royalties.

> ✅ **Correct Answer: B) Prove the core, generate a cash pool, expand and diversify, iterate.**

### Q3. A firm that fails to achieve cost leadership, differentiation, or focus is said to be in what position?
*🟡 Medium | Source: Lecture_W3L32_Competitive_Strategies*

- **D)** Industry innovator

### Q4. What is 'Strategic Adjacency'?
*🟡 Medium | Source: Lecture_W3L29_Industry_and_Business*
