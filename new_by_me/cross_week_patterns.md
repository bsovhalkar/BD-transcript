## 2. 🧩 Common Question Patterns & Formats

### Pattern A: "Which of the following is NOT..."
Very common in the course. The key is identifying the EXCEPTION:
- NOT a demand state → Hyper demand (not real)
- NOT a 10 BD factor → Human Resources
- NOT a disruptive clean technology → BS6
- NOT a characteristic of good research → Impulsive conclusion
- NOT a consumer bonding effort → Charging high prices in shortage

**Strategy:** Rule out what IS correct/real; the remaining option = the answer.

---

### Pattern B: "All of the above"
Many BD questions have "all the above" as the correct answer — often when the question tests a COMPREHENSIVE concept:
- Operations strategy determines: Capacity + Quality + Cost → **All three**
- Effective advertising objectives: Awareness + Trial + Repeat → **All three**
- Factors for alliance success: SCOC → **All four**
- Aspects of culture in negotiation: Agreement + Time + Objectivity → **All three**

**Strategy:** When all options are plausible and describe facets of the same concept, "all the above" is likely correct.

---

### Pattern C: Case-based Company Identification
The course consistently tests knowledge of which company = which innovation/strategy:
- IKEA → minimalist flat-pack furniture
- Toyota → Toyota Production System (TPS)
- Nike → "Just Do It"
- Adani Wilmar → Fortune oil
- Corning → Glass (Gorilla Glass, optical fibre)
- Happiest Minds → Digital services > 95%, L&T MindTree trigger
- Delhivery → Digital backbone, ONDC
- Nirma → FMCG + Cement (non-FMCG) diversification
- Zomato → Cautionary tale (discounts + loss-making)
- Marico → Hindustan Lever hostile attempt on coconut oil

**Strategy:** Memorise company-strategy pairings as flashcards.

---

### Pattern D: Terminological Precision
Questions test whether you know the EXACT term:
- 3D printing = **Additive manufacturing** (not "rapid printing")
- Licensing technology = **Fee and/or royalty** (not free, not acquisition)
- Japanese honorific = **San** (not Esquire, not Sayonara, not Arigato)
- Measuring agreement = **Likert scale** (not happiness scale)
- MNC enters to secure resources = **Security alliance** (not complementarity)

**Strategy:** Memorise the precise technical terms; do not confuse related words.

---

### Pattern E: Distinguishing Similar Demand States
Questions frequently confuse the 8 demand states. Key distinctions:

| Demand State | Definition | Key Differentiator |
|---|---|---|
| **Negative** | Consumers DISLIKE and AVOID | Active avoidance |
| **Non-existent** | Consumers UNAWARE or INDIFFERENT | No awareness/care |
| **Latent** | Strong need but NO product exists | Unexpressed need |
| **Deviant** | Demand for SOCIALLY HARMFUL products | Socially undesirable |
| **Declining** | Losing interest over time | Was there, now fading |
| **Irregular** | Seasonal/cyclical demand | Timing variation |
| **Full** | Perfect supply-demand match | Ideal state |
| **Overfull** | Demand exceeds supply | Too much demand |

<a name="formulas"></a>
## 4. 📐 Frequently Used Formulas & Frameworks

### 4.1 Ansoff Product-Market Growth Matrix

```
                   Existing Product    New Product
Existing Market  | Market Penetration | Product Development |
New Market       | Market Development | Diversification     |
```

**Risk order:** Market Penetration < Product Development < Market Development < Diversification

---

### 4.2 The 8 States of Demand (NNLDIFOD)

1. Negative 2. Non-existent 3. Latent 4. Declining 5. Irregular 6. Full 7. Overfull 8. Deviant

*Mnemonic: "Never Neglect Latent Demand, It Fills Our Drive"*

---

### 4.3 Alliance Success = SCOC Framework

**S**trategic fit + **C**ompetency fit + **O**rganisational fit + **C**ultural fit = All four required

---

### 4.4 The BD Process (Value Chain Fusion)

```
Introspect Company Value Chain
      ↓
Identify Value Points + Value Gaps
      ↓
Find Partner with Complementary Value Chain
      ↓
Test Strategic Feasibility + Economic Viability
      ↓
Create Combined Value Proposition
      ↓
Formalise Partnership Agreement
      ↓
Execute + Monitor
```

---

### 4.5 The Research Spectrum (B-A-D-M)

```
Basic → Applied → Developmental → Market Research
(20+ yrs)  (5-10 yrs)  (1-5 yrs)   (weeks-months)
Pure science → Problem → Prototype → Customer
```

---

### 4.6 Sales Performance Management Cascade

```
Company Mission → Business Strategy → Sales Plan → Execution → Monitoring
```

---

### 4.7 Communication Model (with Feedback)

```
Sender → Encoding → Channel → Decoding → Receiver → FEEDBACK → Sender
                                                        (most ignored)
```

---

### 4.8 Advertising Objectives: ATR Model

```
Awareness → Trial → Repeat Purchase
(AIDA variant: Awareness → Interest → Desire → Action)
```

---

### 4.9 Porter's Five Forces

1. Threat of New Entrants
2. Bargaining Power of Buyers
3. Bargaining Power of Suppliers
4. Threat of Substitutes
5. Industry Rivalry

---

### 4.10 Porter's Value Chain

**Primary Activities:** Inbound Logistics → Operations → Outbound Logistics → Marketing & Sales → Service

**Support Activities:** Procurement | HR | Technology | Firm Infrastructure

<a name="traps"></a>
## 9. ⚠️ Common Traps & Misconceptions

| Trap | Correct Understanding |
|---|---|
| BD = Sales | BD = strategic partnerships (long-term); Sales = transactions (short-term) |
| Negative demand = Non-existent demand | Negative = active dislike; Non-existent = unaware/indifferent |
| Deviant demand = Negative demand | Deviant = socially harmful products demanded; Negative = disliked |
| Marketing = advertising | Marketing is the full market management process; advertising is one tool |
| Likert scale measures happiness | Likert = agreement-disagreement scale |
| Statistical forecasting works under disruption | Disruption invalidates historical data; use expert + simulation |
| Awareness = adoption | Adoption = converting aware to ACTUAL users (behavior change, not just knowledge) |
| Size/market share protects against disruption | Kodak/Nokia/Blackberry disprove this; continuous innovation is the real defence |
| Technical collaboration = acquisition | Tech collab = temporary license (fee/royalty); acquisition = permanent ownership |
| BS6 = disruptive clean technology | BS6 = incremental ICE improvement; disruptive = EV, Green H2, Fuel Cell |
| Humira is a biosimilar | Humira IS the original biologic; biosimilars are copies (Amjevita, etc.) |
| LED = LCD | LED = lighting Nobel Prize tech; LCD = display technology |
| 3D printing = rapid printing | 3D printing = Additive Manufacturing (technically precise term) |
| Strategy alone ensures alliance success | ALL four fits (SCOC) needed; cultural misalignment kills strategically sound alliances |
| Sales performance starts with field execution | It starts with Mission → Strategy → Sales Plan, THEN execution |
| Salesforce size = salesforce effectiveness | Effectiveness = training quality and capability, not headcount |
| IKEA = restaurants | IKEA's BD = minimalist flat-pack furniture; restaurants are a support feature |
| Fortune oil = Marico | Fortune = Adani Wilmar; Saffola = Marico |
| Coca-Cola = diversified portfolio | Coca-Cola built global scale largely on its core carbonated beverage |
| Delhivery competitive edge = trucks | Delhivery's edge = digital backbone (tech platform) |
| Zomato = success story | Zomato = cautionary tale of loss-funded market share building |
